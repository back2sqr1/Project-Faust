\section{Orthogonality}

\begin{definition}
  A projector is a matrix $P$ such that $P^2 = P$.
\end{definition}

An oblique projector is a matrix $P$ such that $P^2 = P$ but $P^TP \neq I$.

From a geometric point of view, the projector might arise
from the notion that if one were to shine a light onto the subspace 
$range(P)$, the light would then cast a shadow on $Pv$ projected by an arbitrary vector $v$.

\begin{definition}
  A complimentary projector is a matrix $(I - P)$ such that $(I - P)^2 = I - P$.
  This is also the nullspace of P.

  In addition
  \begin{align*}
    range(I - P) = null(P) \\
    null(I - P) = range(P)
  \end{align*}

  A projector separates a vector space into two subspaces the nullspace and the range.
\end{definition}
